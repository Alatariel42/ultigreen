Ways to Reduce Your Carbon Footprint

There are a lot of very surprising ways that you can easily cut down the carbon footprint you produce with dining decisions (or packed food). Here are just a few:

  • Eat Vegan / Vegetarian

    The carbon footprint created by eating meat is significantly greater than eating a healthy, protein-packed Vegan meal. Additionally, the water required to sustain a vegetarian diet is half that of a meat-based diet. That way you can conserve water without actively taking shorter showers!

  • Eat Organic Food

    Organic farming methods for both crops and animals have a much lower impact on the environment than conventional methods simply because of the use of natural pesticides, removal of growth of growth hormones, and natural soil fertilization methods.

  • Shop Locally

    A large portion of the carbon footprint created by food is in the packaging and shipment of the food to your grocery store. Imagine all CO2 you can reduce by buying fruits and veggies grown in your backyard.

  • Eat at the Cafeteria

    Not only will you be cutting down on the CO2 you create by driving to get food, you will also have the option to utilize the healthy meals that are both good for you, the environment, and your wallet.
